Story Map Process

Using the ESRI Storymap site we created a three part story map (Beginning, Middle and End). Due in part to the 30 point limit this did work in our favour as to not bombard the user with data points flooding over a small area of the map.

Step One

By clicking on the plus icon at the bottom of the page, we can then see a list of choices to choose from, Guided tour (beta) is what was chosen.

Step Two

After inserting the Guided tour (beta) we added 5 slides for the top 5 coordinates of each part of the 2-month journey accordingly. These will need to be updated to include the rest at a later date.

Step Three

From here we could add in our historic data research of Treaty 9 to our liking.

Step Four

In order to place the coordinates it was a simple matter of selecting "Edit Location" on the slide.

Step Five

A new window appeared, allowing us to type in the supplied coordinates of our client into the top left of the search bar. We could then simply click on the location where we wanted to place the coordinate, or click and drag the symbol if we were unsatisfied with its location. Doing this saved a lot of time as we were able to zoom in on our geographic locations quickly and place down the markers.

Step Six

We could then change the colour of the symbols within the Design tab. The site itself recommends 3 as shown below, or you can pick your own. For this project we chose to pick from one of the recommended colours.
